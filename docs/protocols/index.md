## The Four Protocol Areas

### [:material-food-apple: Anti-Inflammatory Diet](anti-inflammatory-diet.md)

The foundation of everything else. Food is information—it tells your cells how to behave. The wrong foods perpetuate inflammation; the right foods help resolve it.

**Key changes:**

- Elimination of inflammatory triggers (processed foods, sugar, gluten, dairy)
- Addition of anti-inflammatory powerhouses (fatty fish, colorful vegetables, turmeric)
- Strategic meal timing and preparation
- Sustainable long-term eating patterns

**Timeline:** Noticeable improvement in 2–3 weeks; significant changes by 6–8 weeks.

---

### [:material-pill: Supplement Protocol](supplements.md)

Targeted nutritional support to address deficiencies and accelerate healing. Not replacements for good food—additions that help the body do what it's trying to do.

**Core supplements:**

- Omega-3 fatty acids (anti-inflammatory foundation)
- Vitamin D (immune regulation, severely deficient in most people)
- Curcumin/Turmeric (nature's NSAID)
- Magnesium (hundreds of enzymatic processes)
- Additional targeted support based on testing

**Approach:** Test, don't guess. Address documented deficiencies first.

---

### [:material-cannabis: Cannabis as Medicine](cannabis.md)

For many with chronic inflammation and pain, medical cannabis offers relief that pharmaceutical options cannot match, often with fewer side effects.

**Key distinctions:**

- Medical vs. recreational use (completely different approaches)
- CBD vs. THC (different compounds, different purposes)
- Dosing strategies (start low, go slow)
- Legal and practical considerations

**Important:** Not for everyone. Legal status varies. Requires informed, careful approach.

---

### [:material-mushroom: Medicinal Mushrooms](mushrooms.md)

The irony of Bram's journey: poisoned by one fungus, healed by others. Medicinal mushrooms have thousands of years of traditional use and growing research support.

**Key species:**

- Reishi — Immune modulation, sleep, inflammation
- Lion's Mane — Cognitive function, nerve support
- Cordyceps — Energy, endurance, mitochondrial support
- Chaga — Antioxidant powerhouse
- Turkey Tail — Immune balancing

**Approach:** Start simple, quality matters enormously, consistency is key.

## Protocol Timing and Sequencing

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1–4)
**Priority: Environment + Basic Nutrition**

1. Address environmental toxin exposure (mold, etc.)
2. Eliminate obvious inflammatory foods
3. Begin basic anti-inflammatory eating
4. Start core supplements (Omega-3, Vitamin D, Magnesium)

### Phase 2: Expansion (Weeks 4–8)
**Priority: Complete Protocol Implementation**

1. Full elimination diet in place
2. Complete supplement protocol running
3. Add medicinal mushrooms (start with one species)
4. Consider medical cannabis if appropriate
5. Daily movement protocols established

### Phase 3: Optimization (Months 2–6)
**Priority: Fine-Tuning and Personalization**

1. Retest inflammatory markers
2. Adjust supplements based on response
3. Expand mushroom protocol
4. Refine diet based on individual response
5. Dial in what works specifically for you

### Phase 4: Maintenance (Ongoing)
**Priority: Sustainable Long-Term Practice**

1. 80/20 approach to diet (flexible but foundational)
2. Core supplements continue
3. Mushrooms as daily practice
4. Movement protocols automatic
5. Periodic reassessment

## The Non-Negotiables

Regardless of which specific protocols you choose, certain principles are universal:

!!! success "Universal Recovery Principles"
    
    1. **Remove the source of ongoing damage** (mold, toxins, inflammatory foods)
    
    2. **Give the body raw materials to heal** (nutrients, building blocks)
    
    3. **Reduce inflammation actively** (diet, supplements, lifestyle)
    
    4. **Move the body appropriately** (mobility, stability, circulation)
    
    5. **Allow recovery time** (sleep, stress management, patience)
    
    6. **Stay consistent over months** (not days or weeks)

## A Note on Cost

Quality supplements and organic food cost money. Bram spent approximately:

| Category | Monthly Cost |
|----------|--------------|
| Supplements | $150–200 |
| Premium food (above regular groceries) | $100–150 |
| Medical cannabis (where legal) | $50–100 |
| Medicinal mushrooms | $50–75 |
| **Total additional** | **$350–525/month** |

This is significant. But consider the alternative costs:

- Lost work time from illness
- Medical appointments and treatments
- Potential surgery
- Long-term medication
- Reduced quality of life

Prevention and recovery investment often costs less than disease management.

**If budget is tight:** Prioritize food quality first, then core supplements. Mushrooms and cannabis are valuable but not essential. Many people recover with just diet, basic supplements, and movement.
